What is Jiu Jitsu?

Brazilian Jiu Jitsu is a complete martial art — from the moment two people face each other standing, to the ground where the real game begins. It covers takedowns, transitions, and submissions, all built on technique and awareness rather than size or strength.

Students learn to stay calm under pressure and find solutions in real time. For children, that means developing focus, patience, and problem-solving — built through hard work and consistency, class after class.

Your child will learn more than Jiu Jitsu.

At Zaandam Jiu Jitsu Club, we believe the mat is a place where children discover who they are. Through jiu jitsu, they learn to move, to think, and to solve problems under pressure.

Without realising it, they are also learning to persist, to trust themselves, and to handle hard things with calm.

What they build here, they carry everywhere, into school, into friendships, into life. Not because we tell them to. Because they lived it every class, every week.

Who is Danillo Bighi?

Danillo was born and raised in Rio de Janeiro, the birthplace of Brazilian Jiu Jitsu. He has been training since 2004 and received his black belt from Roger Gracie, the greatest jiu jitsu competitor of all time.

A coach and team leader with over two decades on the mat, Danillo has worked with students of all ages and levels.

The son of two teachers, Danillo grew up understanding the value of good instruction. When he began working with children and saw how profoundly jiu jitsu could shape them, not just as athletes, but as people, he never looked back. He has led strong children's programmes in both Rio de Janeiro and Amsterdam, and his focus is now fully in Zaandam.
